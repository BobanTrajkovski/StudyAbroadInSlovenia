Travelling around Slovenia





Slovenia is a country with a lot of beautiful sights mountains and landscapes. It is the country that whole world compares to Swizerland and it is the third country by most trees on the land.

There are a lot of beautiful destinations in Slovenia, but the best best to visit are Bled, Ljubljana, Maribor and Koper.

Bled is a city known for the lake Bled which is the pearl of Slovenia... Ljubljana is the capital with really beautiful sights that are the Ljubljana castle, river Ljubljanica and the Dragon bridge... Maribor is well known for a place that is located near mountains with cold weather and amazing landscapes. It is mostly known for the Austro-Hungarian buildings... Koper is the city that goes out on sea with beautiful beaches and a lot of tourists. It is known for the old Italian Style buildings...



How to reach this cities?

Moving around Slovenia is relatively easy, as the country has a well-developed transportation infrastructure. Here are some options for getting around Slovenia:

Public transportation:

Slovenia has a network of buses, trains, and local transit systems that connect most of the major cities and towns. The buses and trains are generally reliable, affordable, and safe. Students can buy tickets at the stations or online.

Car rental:

Car rental is available in Slovenia, and it is a good option if you want to explore the countryside or visit remote areas. However, driving in Slovenia can be challenging due to the winding roads and mountainous terrain. Students will need a valid driving license and insurance to rent a car.

Cycling:

Cycling is a popular way to explore Slovenia, especially in the summer months. The country has a well-developed network of bike paths and trails that offer stunning views of the countryside. Students can rent bikes from local shops or use the city bike-sharing programs.

Down below there is a link for renting a bike in Slovenia:

Rentabike.si


Hitchhiking:

Hitchhiking is a common practice in Slovenia, especially among young travelers. However, it is not the safest option, and students should take necessary precautions if they choose to hitchhike.


Walking:

Walking is a great way to explore the cities and towns of Slovenia. Most of the major attractions and landmarks are located within walking distance of each other, and the country has a well-developed pedestrian infrastructure. Overall, students have plenty of options for moving around Slovenia, and they can choose the one that best suits their needs and budget. This transports are connecting the students with all around cities and students can have really easy and cheap transportation.
